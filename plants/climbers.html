Support Seekers

Climbers have very thin, long, and weak stems. They cannot stand upright on their own and use external support like walls, sticks, or other plants to grow vertically.

Mechanism: They use special structures called tendrils to hook onto things.

Examples: Money plant, Pea plant, and Grapevine.

🌱 Did you know?

Climbers grow much faster than other plants because they don't waste energy building thick trunks; they just use others for support!
